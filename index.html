Facts about the Moon

Moon Facts

The Moon has always fascinated me. Not just because it lights up the night sky, but because it represents how much we still don’t know. It feels both close and impossibly far away at the same time. Growing up, people have always looked at the Moon with curiosity, and now, with missions like Artemis II, we are finally returning to explore it again after decades since Apollo 17. To me, this makes the Moon feel more real and more important—it’s not just something distant, but a place we are reaching for once again. It reminds me that no matter how much we discover, there is always more waiting beyond our world.From its surprising features to the missions that have explored it, the Moon is full of fascinating facts that make it even more incredible.

  1. The Moon is drifting away
    • It moves about 1.5 inches farther from Earth every year
  2. Only 12 people have walked on the Moon.
    • All of them were part of the Apollo program missions.
  3. A day on the Moon lasts 29.5 Earth days
    • That’s how long it takes to go from one full moon to the next.
  4. The temperature is extreme
    • It can go from 260°F (day) to -280°F (night)
